Hamas Responds to Trump Plan, Rejects Immediate Disarmament

Hamas responded to President Trump's Gaza peace plan, agreeing in principle to release all Israeli hostages and hand over Gaza administration to an independent Palestinian body, but refusing demands for immediate disarmament or exclusion from future Palestinian governance. The group stated willingness to negotiate details and seeks complete IDF withdrawal from Gaza. Washington and Jerusalem interpreted Hamas's refusal to address disarmament as a de facto rejection of the plan's core. Hamas officials in Doha sought changes including keeping "defensive" weapons, setting Israeli withdrawal timelines, and clarifying peacekeeper roles.

Trump stated "Based on the just published statement by Hamas, I believe they are ready for a long peace.
